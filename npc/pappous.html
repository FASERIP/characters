Pappous Kostas

Konstantinos Theódoros "Pappous Kostas" Athanasiou is Stella Blake's maternal grandfather. He immigrated to New York City from Peloponnese, Greece as a child. Kostas worked as a fisherman, as his father did, when school wasn't in session and after graduation. When World War II broke out, Kostas enlisted in the Navy and was a radioman on the USS New York (BB-34). After the war he married Maria Teresa Aiello, and continued to work as a fisherman until they'd saved enough money to open the restaurant and grocery in 1947.

A hale, robust man of great height and heroic frame, Pappous Kostas's wave of black hair has turned grey with years. Jovial and sanguine, Pappous has a twinkle of mischief from his youth that never left and is a virtuoso on the bouzouki. He's the family's heart, the rock on which everyone else depends. In addition to the restaurant, Pappous runs the Kratos Athletic Club, a gym that focuses on boxing, Greco-Roman Wrestling, gymnastics, and weights, featuring a landmark traditional Turkish bath in the back. The apartments above he rents below market to young people in need. He also owns and operates the Kalliopi's Arcade at the South Street Pier. His oldest children, Giannis and Andreas, have recently taken over the other family interests such as apartments, parking lots, and the expansion locations of the specialty grocery/bodega portion of the original business.
